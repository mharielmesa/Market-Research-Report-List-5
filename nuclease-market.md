<p><p>The nuclease market is segmented into two main types: DNases and RNases. DNases target deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA), playing a crucial role in applications like genetic research and molecular biology, where they facilitate DNA manipulation and degradation. RNases, on the other hand, act on ribonucleic acid (RNA) and are essential in processes such as RNA analysis and transcript degradation. Together, these enzymes are vital in various sectors, including pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, and diagnostics, supporting advancements in genetic studies and therapeutics.</p></p>
