<p><p>The PP nonwoven fabrics market is categorized into several types, including spunbonded, staples, composite, and meltblown fabrics. Spunbonded fabrics are created by directly bonding polypropylene fibers, offering durability and versatility for various applications. Staple fabrics utilize shorter fiber lengths, providing a softer texture ideal for hygiene products. Composite fabrics combine different nonwoven technologies for enhanced functionality. Meltblown fabrics, known for their fine fibers, are crucial in filtration and medical materials due to their high-performance characteristics.</p></p>
