<p><p>Cord blood banking services encompass three main market types: private banks, public banks, and hybrid banks. Private banks collect and store umbilical cord blood for individual families, offering exclusive benefits for personal use. Public banks, on the other hand, accept donations and make cord blood available for research and public transplant needs, promoting broader societal benefit. Hybrid banks combine elements of both, allowing families to choose between private storage and public donation options, thus maximizing both personal and communal value.</p></p>
