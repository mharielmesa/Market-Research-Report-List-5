<p><ul><li>Monomer Polymerization</li><li>Resin Degradation</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Micronized PTFE market comprises two primary types: Monomer Polymerization and Resin Degradation. Monomer Polymerization involves the synthesis of polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE) from raw monomers, resulting in finely divided PTFE particles that enhance properties like thermal stability and chemical resistance. Resin Degradation, on the other hand, involves breaking down larger PTFE resin into micron-sized particles, improving processability and dispersibility for various applications. Both types cater to diverse industries, including coatings, lubricants, and plastics, driving demand for specialized formulations.</p></p>
