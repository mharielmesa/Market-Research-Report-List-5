<p><p>Network-on-Chip (NoC) is an advanced communication framework designed for integrated circuits, particularly in multi-core processing environments. The NoC market can be categorized into two main topology types: direct and indirect. Direct topology features dedicated connections between nodes, offering efficient and low-latency communication but limited scalability. In contrast, indirect topology utilizes shared resources and communication channels, enhancing scalability and flexibility at the cost of increased latency. Both types cater to different application needs, influencing market dynamics and technology adoption.</p></p>
