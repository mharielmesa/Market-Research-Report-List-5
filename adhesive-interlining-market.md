<p><p>Adhesive interlining is a fabric used to reinforce and stabilize garments, enhancing structure and durability. The market includes PA (Polyamide) adhesive interlining, known for its strong bonding and heat resistance, ideal for high-temperature applications. PE (Polyethylene) adhesive interlining offers a softer feel and lower melting point, suitable for lightweight fabrics. Other types include EVA (Ethylene Vinyl Acetate) and TPU (Thermoplastic Polyurethane), catering to diverse textile needs. Each type varies in bonding strength, flexibility, and application suitability, meeting specific garment manufacturing requirements.</p></p>
