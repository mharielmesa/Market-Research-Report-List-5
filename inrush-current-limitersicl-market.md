<p><p>Inrush Current Limiters (ICLs) are devices designed to manage the initial surge of current when electrical equipment is powered on. The market for ICLs is primarily categorized into three types: </p><p>1. NTC (Negative Temperature Coefficient) ICLs, which decrease resistance as temperature rises, allowing for controlled current flow during startup.  </p><p>2. PTC (Positive Temperature Coefficient) ICLs, which increase resistance with temperature, effectively limiting excessive current.  </p><p>3. Other types may include thermistors and resistive devices, which serve similar functions in managing inrush currents in various applications.</p></p>
