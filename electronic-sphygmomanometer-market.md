<p><p>The electronic sphygmomanometer market is primarily divided into two types: upper arm electronic sphygmomanometers and wrist electronic sphygmomanometers. Upper arm models are typically more accurate and favored for clinical use, utilizing a cuff that wraps around the bicep. In contrast, wrist models are compact and portable, ideal for on-the-go monitoring, though they may be less precise. Both types cater to varying consumer needs, with upper arm models often preferred for home or medical settings and wrist devices for convenience.</p></p>
